Ingedients

  • 600 g / 1.2 lb beef mince (ground beef)

  • 1/8 tsp of salt and pepper

  • 2-3 tbsp canola oil

  • 2 soft burger buns or rolls

  • 1-2 slices of cheese (your preference)

  • 1 large tomato

  • Lettuce

  • Any other extra ingredients such as (pickles, onions, etc)

Recipe

  1. Seperate the beef into 1-4 equal portions (depending how much you want), roll them into balls, then press down to make round patties that is slightly larger than the bun

  2. Preheat the grill to high and place the bread cut face up on a tray and toast it for 3-5 minutes until there is a light golden color. Set aside

  3. Sprinkle one side of the patty with half the salt and pepper. Flip the burger then press a dent into the middle of each patty and sprinkle the dented side with salt and pepper.

  4. Preheat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large cast iron skillet over high heat until it gets very hot

  5. Cook the patties for 1 1/2 minutes. Press down lightly with a spatula and when ready flip the burger.

  6. Immediately place a slice of cheese of your choosing on top of the patty and cook for another 1 1/2 minutes.

  7. Once finished, trasnfer the burgers to a tray to rest for 3 minutes.

  8. Assemble the burgers with the buns, tomatos, lettuce, and whatever other ingredients you would like to add.

Ingedients

  • 1 lb of fettuccine noodles

  • 1 stick of butter

  • 1 cup of heavy cream

  • Salt and papper

  • 2 cups of freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Recipe

  1. Cook the pasta according to package directions

  2. In a saucepan or skillet, warm the butter and cream and season it with salt and pepper

  3. Grate the parmesan cheese and place half of it into a large serving bowl. Pour warm butter/cream mixture over the top

  4. Drain the pasta and immediately place the pasta into the bowl

  5. Toss the pasta in the bowl well enough then sprinkle in the other half of the parmesan cheese.

  6. If you'd like, combine the alfredo pasta with meat or salad of your choosing.

Ingedients

  • 1 lb ground beef

  • 2 cubes beef bouillion

  • 1 can tomato sauce 8 oz

  • 1 can tomato paste 6 oz

  • pepper to taste

  • 2 cups hot water

  • 2 teaspoon sugar

  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil

  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano

  • dash of garlic

  • 16 oz spaghetti noodles

Recipe

  1. Brown the groundbeef in a large pan and drain the fat if you wish to do so.

  2. Once cooked, throw in salt, pepper, tomato sauce and paste, water (with the bouillon cubes in it), sugar, basil, oregano and garlic. Simmer on low for an hour.

  3. A few minutes before the hour is done, cook box of spaghetti noodles as directed on package.

  4. Once the noodles are cooked, drain and add to spaghetti sauce.

Ingedients

  • 16 ounces pizza dough, store-bought or homemade

  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce

  • 18 to 20 slices pepperoni

  • 12 ounces mozzarella cheese, grated

  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

  • 1 teaspoon fresh oregano, optional

  • Flour for rolling and shaping dough

Recipe

  1. Preheart the oven to 500Â°F

  2. Roll out dough on a lightly floured surface into about a 14-inch diameter circle.

  3. Transfer the dough to a lightly dusted pizza peel. Alternatively, fit it into a large cast-iron.

  4. Add sauce in a light layer all over the pizza. Leave about 1/4 inch crust around the edges

  5. Lay out the pepperoni slices and grated cheese on top of the sauce and season with black pepper.

  6. Cook for 6 minutes, then rotate the pizza halfway so it cooks evenly. Cook for another 6-8 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and charred in spots.

  7. Let the pizza rest for a minute and slice into pieces.
